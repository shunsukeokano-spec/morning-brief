Trump-Xi Summit Ends Without AI Deal — H200 Clearance Quietly Issued Anyway ↗
Trump's Beijing summit (May 14–15) produced no formal agreement on AI or chip exports, with US Trade Representative Greer confirming chip export controls were not discussed at the bilateral meeting. Yet Reuters reported Washington cleared Nvidia H200 sales to ~10 Chinese firms including Alibaba, Tencent, and ByteDance, capped at 75,000 units per customer — a move a CFR senior fellow called 'politically explosive.' As of this writing, Commerce Secretary Lutnick confirmed no H200s have actually shipped to China, leaving the deal in legal and diplomatic limbo.
↳ Trump's China tech policy is now fully transactional — chip access is being traded for broader economic concessions — but execution is paralyzed between congressional hawks and Beijing's own chip-nationalism drive, making this the defining uncertainty for AI hardware markets heading into H2 2026.
Asia · South China Morning Post Your Focus ↓
China's Chip Independence Accelerates: DeepSeek Runs on Huawei, Beijing Urges Firms to Reject Nvidia ↗
Beijing is running a deliberate two-track strategy: accepting H200 clearances at the diplomatic level while simultaneously urging domestic tech firms to reject US chips. DeepSeek pivoted to Huawei's Ascend chips for inference workloads, and Huawei's secretive Chip Fundamental Technology Research Laboratory was featured on prime-time state TV (CCTV) — a rare public signal of Beijing's semiconductor ambitions — just ahead of the Trump visit. Tencent and Alibaba both flagged increased domestic chip availability on their Q1 earnings calls.
↳ China is engineering strategic optionality — publicly 'opening' to US chips while hardening domestic supply chains — meaning any Nvidia China revenue recovery will be slower and smaller than markets currently price in.
Asia · Japan Times / Bloomberg World ↑
Kioxia Announces US Listing After Record $8.2B Quarterly Profit — 2026 NAND Already 'Sold Out' ↗
Japanese flash memory giant Kioxia — up 300% YTD and the world's best-performing major stock this year — announced plans to list American depositary shares on a US exchange. The company posted record earnings of ¥596.8 billion for Q1 FY2026 (surpassing Toyota) and forecast ¥1.3 trillion in operating profit for Q2, far above analyst estimates. Kioxia has declared its entire 2026 NAND production capacity 'sold out', with tightness projected to extend into 2027, as hyperscaler AI infrastructure spending drives insatiable storage demand.
↳ NAND flash is emerging as the next critical AI infrastructure bottleneck after GPUs; Kioxia's US listing will bring a new, structurally-important AI supply-chain play into the direct line of sight of US institutional investors at the peak of the shortage.
Western · Yahoo Finance / GuruFocus World ↓
Semiconductor Rally Pauses on Inflation Shock — Analysts Warn of 1999-Style Correction Risk ↗
Chip stocks sold off sharply on Friday (May 15) after a hotter-than-expected US CPI print (3.8% YoY, fastest since May 2023) raised fears of prolonged high rates. Nvidia, AMD, Marvell, and Micron each fell 3–5%, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index dropped over 3% — despite having surged ~64% since late March. BTIG analyst Jonathan Krinsky warned the magnitude of the semiconductor markup 'in some ways is actually more extreme' than the dot-com bubble, flagging a potential 25–30% correction.
↳ The convergence of macro inflation risk, the Trump-Xi summit's failure to deliver a clean chip-deal catalyst, and stretched semiconductor valuations creates a fragile setup for the sector entering the week of May 18.
