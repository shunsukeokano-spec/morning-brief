Trump-Xi Summit: Strategic Stability Rhetoric, Zero Breakthroughs on the Hard Issues ↗
Trump's two-day Beijing visit (May 13–15) was long on pageantry and short on substance. Both sides agreed to frame the relationship as 'constructive strategic stability,' but Reuters reported Trump left with no major breakthroughs on trade or Iran. Xi delivered an unequivocal warning on Taiwan, while Trump — in a notable shift — told Fox News he was 'not looking to have somebody go independent,' signaling a softened US stance that alarmed Taipei. Xi has been invited to visit Washington on September 24.
↳ The summit's real output is geopolitical: Xi secured a tacit US endorsement against Taiwan independence, while Trump got pageantry and a Boeing order — a structurally asymmetric outcome with lasting implications for Indo-Pacific security.
Western · CNBC / Time Your Focus ↓
AI Chips Were the Elephant in the Room at Beijing — and Beijing Chose Not to Buy ↗
Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang flew to Beijing on Air Force One, hoping to unlock H200 chip sales to Chinese firms including Alibaba, Tencent, and ByteDance. Washington quietly cleared sales to ~10 Chinese firms, but chip export controls were not formally discussed at the bilateral meeting. Crucially, Beijing has signaled reluctance to become dependent on US-regulated chips, with Nomura's chief China economist calling it 'a calculated defensive maneuver' to protect China's own AI chipmakers. No H200 deliveries have been made and Nvidia has booked zero China revenue.
↳ The US chip export lever — long seen as Washington's key technological chokepoint over China — is losing effectiveness as China's domestic AI stack matures, directly reshaping the strategic logic of export controls.
Western · Reuters Your Focus ↓
DeepSeek V4 Runs on Huawei Chips: China's AI Stack Is Going Indigenous ↗
DeepSeek launched a preview of its V4 model trained on Huawei's Ascend chips in late April, marking a pivotal shift away from Nvidia hardware. The Pro version outperforms open-source rivals and tops Hugging Face rankings. Analysts at Omdia called it 'a big deal for China's AI industry,' while Nvidia CEO Huang warned that 'the day that DeepSeek comes out on Huawei first, that is a horrible outcome for our nation.' This development directly undermines the strategic rationale for US chip export controls — if China can build frontier models on domestic silicon, denying Nvidia access hurts US revenue more than it constrains Chinese AI capability.
↳ China's accelerating hardware-software co-integration means the window for US chip controls to act as a meaningful AI capability brake is closing faster than Washington's policy cycle can respond.
Western · S&P Global Market Intelligence / Motley Fool Your Focus ◉
Nvidia Reports Q1 FY2027 Earnings Today — China Is the Highest-Variance Variable ↗
Nvidia reports fiscal Q1 2027 earnings after the US market close today (May 20), with Wall Street expecting ~$78–79 billion in revenue and ~80% year-on-year EPS growth. The print is the single largest AI infrastructure data point this quarter. Critically, Nvidia's guidance has excluded China entirely since H20 restrictions took effect, meaning any constructive signal on H200 sales to Alibaba, Tencent, or ByteDance would unlock incremental revenue not in current consensus — while any new restriction would compress estimates sharply. The stock reaction is expected to be significant in either direction.
↳ Nvidia's guidance commentary on China tonight will be the most consequential real-time read on whether the Trump-Xi summit produced any tangible technology thaw — making it both a market event and a geopolitical signal.
30–90 Day Signal
Watch three converging threads over the next 30–90 days: (1) Whether H200 deliveries to Chinese firms actually materialize before Xi's September 24 Washington visit — any shipment confirmation resets Nvidia's China revenue model from zero and triggers analyst upgrades across the semiconductor sector. (2) China's domestic chip stack trajectory: DeepSeek V4's Huawei-native training is a proof-of-concept; if V4 reaches full production on Ascend hardware by Q3, the US export control argument collapses politically in Washington. (3) Taiwan: Trump's 'no commitment either way' framing post-summit is the most ambiguous US Taiwan stance in decades — watch for PLA military activity around the strait and Taiwanese government responses as the real stress-test of what was (or wasn't) agreed in Beijing.
