Western · Bloomberg / Taipei Times Your Focus ◉
Taiwan Weighs Criminal Ban on AI Chip Exports to All of China ↗
Taiwan is considering far stricter export controls that would restrict AI chip sales to every customer in China — not only blacklisted firms like Huawei — and criminalize smuggling for the first time, according to Bloomberg (June 9). The measures are being discussed as part of ongoing US–Taiwan trade talks, with rules likely mirroring Washington's processing-power threshold approach. If enacted, it would be the boldest move yet by President Lai's administration, aligning Taiwan firmly with US containment strategy and potentially drawing sharp retaliation from Beijing.
↳ This directly reshapes the global AI chip supply chain, closes a major loophole enabling semiconductor smuggling, and escalates Taiwan's strategic alignment with the US — with major implications for Japan's own export control posture and cross-strait stability.
Western · CNBC World ↓
Broadcom Miss Wipes $1.3 Trillion from Global Chip Sector in One Day ↗
On June 5, the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index cratered over 6% and the Nasdaq fell 4% after Broadcom's Q3 AI chip sales guidance of $16 billion fell short of the $17.2 billion analyst estimate — despite AI revenue growing 143% year-over-year. AMD dropped nearly 11%, Intel fell over 11%, and Nvidia briefly lost its $5 trillion valuation crown. Analysts are split on whether the miss signals genuine AI demand cooling or simply expectations that ran too far ahead of reality.
↳ The first major stress test of the AI chip investment thesis in 2026 — reveals extreme valuation sensitivity and raises the question of whether AI hardware spending is beginning to plateau, which would ripple across Japan's electronics and component exporters.
Western · Fortune / OpenAI World ◉
OpenAI Files Confidential S-1 — IPO Race Is Now a Three-Way Run with Anthropic and SpaceX ↗
OpenAI announced on June 8 that it had confidentially filed an S-1 with the SEC, targeting a potential public listing as early as September 2026 at a valuation of $730B–$850B, with Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and JPMorgan as underwriters. The move follows Anthropic's own confidential S-1 filed June 1 at a $965B valuation, and SpaceX's ongoing IPO roadshow. OpenAI reported $20B+ in annual recurring revenue for 2025 but is projecting a $14 billion loss in 2026 and does not expect profitability until 2029.
↳ The simultaneous public-market debut of OpenAI, Anthropic, and SpaceX will be the definitive valuation test for the AI era — forcing the first-ever public disclosure of frontier AI unit economics and potentially repricing the entire sector.

China's Tech Supply Chain Quietly Dominates Global AI Infrastructure ↗
Xinhua reported on June 7 that Chinese firms now hold seven of the world's top-10 optical module vendor spots, with over 60% global market share, while China exported 117 billion integrated circuits worth $103.5B in just the first four months of 2026. China produced 127.2 billion ICs in Q1 2026, up 24.3% year-on-year. Even as the US tightens export controls on advanced AI chips, China has locked in a dominant position in the photonics and lower-node chip layers essential to global AI data center buildout.
↳ Highlights the paradox at the heart of US chip containment: while advanced logic chips are being restricted, China's grip on the optical and legacy-node supply chain means any global AI infrastructure buildout still flows through Chinese suppliers — a strategic vulnerability for Japan and the West.
