EU Ratifies Fragile US Trade Deal — But Distrust Is Structural ↗
The EU Council and European Parliament struck a provisional deal to implement the Turnberry Agreement's tariff terms, with the EU scrapping tariffs on US industrial goods in exchange for a 15% US tariff cap on most European goods. However, the pact expires December 2029 and includes a suspension mechanism if the US fails to lift steel and aluminum tariffs above 15% by end-2026. A survey in the five largest EU countries found 77% believe the deal 'mostly favors the American economy' and 52% call it 'a humiliation' — with anti-American consumer boycotts now measurable across Europe.
↳ The deal stabilizes transatlantic trade short-term but its built-in fragility — tied to Trump's tariff behavior and a 2029 sunset — means EU-US economic relations remain structurally unstable for the next 3+ years.

Putin-Xi Beijing Summit: A Dollar-Free Axis Accelerates ↗
Putin's 25th visit to China produced 40 cooperation agreements, a 47-page joint declaration on multipolar world order, and a formal push to align China's 15th Five-Year Plan with Russia's 2030 strategy. Bilateral trade grew 20% in the first four months of 2026, with over 99% now settled in rubles and yuan. The relationship is deeply asymmetric: Russia imports over 90% of sanctioned Western technology via Chinese suppliers, while China buys discounted Russian energy — giving Beijing leverage Putin cannot escape.
↳ China is not 'joining Russia's camp' — it is absorbing Russia as a dependent junior partner in a yuan-centric economic bloc, with permanent infrastructure (pipelines, logistics, payment rails) being embedded right now that will outlast any political shift.

BOJ Trapped: Hawks Outnumbered, Yen Near Intervention Line ↗
The Bank of Japan kept its policy rate at 0.75% at its April meeting in a 6-3 vote, with three dissenting members calling for an immediate hike to 1.0%. The BOJ raised its FY2026 core inflation forecast sharply to 2.8% (from 1.9%) on Iran-war energy shock pressures, while cutting its growth forecast to just 0.5%. The yen has weakened past 159 against the dollar — approaching the 160 'line in the sand' — and Japan's Ministry of Finance has already intervened at least once during Golden Week.
↳ Japan faces a rare stagflation trap: hiking rates to defend the yen risks crushing an already fragile 0.5% growth economy, while inaction accelerates import-cost inflation — the next BOJ meeting is a critical trigger point for JPY direction.

Europe's Trump Fatigue Is Becoming an Economic Realignment ↗
Trump's aggressive trade posture is fracturing transatlantic ties at both the business and consumer level. There is currently no EU country where a majority of citizens holds a positive view of the US, according to a YouGov poll. European companies are capitalizing on this shift — notably, the EU awarded a €180 million sovereign cloud contract to a European consortium rather than a US provider. Meanwhile, the US is now Europe's largest export market at over 20% of EU exports, making decoupling economically painful even as political will for it grows.
↳ The EU's strategic push toward 'open strategic autonomy' — in cloud, defense, and trade — is accelerating faster than markets are pricing; this creates medium-term headwinds for US tech and financial firms operating in Europe.
30–90 Day Signal
