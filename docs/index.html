China Certifies Nine Homegrown AI Chips for State Procurement — First-Ever 'AI Inference' Category Created ↗
China's official security bodies certified nine domestically designed AI processors — including Huawei's Ascend 910, Alibaba's T-Head Zhenwu, and chips from Biren, Moore Threads, and others — for government and state-enterprise procurement, creating a brand-new 'AI training and inference chips' category under the Anke security framework. Chinese domestic firms already delivered 41% of local AI server shipments in 2025, with Huawei projecting $12B in AI processor revenue for 2026 alone.
↳ This is China converting industrial policy into guaranteed procurement demand — turning a defensive decoupling into an aggressive domestic capacity-building exercise that will reshape the global AI chip market by 2030.

AI Data Center Supply Chain Hits Critical Bottleneck: Optical Interconnect Demand Now '3x Capacity' ↗
Semtech CEO Hong Hou warned on the company's Q1 earnings call (reported May 28) that demand for key optical networking components — spanning 800G, 1.6T, LPO, and laser products — is now outpacing manufacturing capacity by approximately 3x, as hyperscalers race to build ever-larger AI clusters. Separately, the AI memory super-cycle has already nearly doubled server DRAM prices, with downstream effects hitting smartphones, gaming consoles, and consumer electronics globally.
↳ The bottleneck has structurally shifted from GPUs to the components that connect and feed them — optical interconnects and HBM memory — meaning the next 6–12 months of AI infrastructure buildout will be gated by supply chains largely concentrated in Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan.

Europe's China Sentiment: Paralysis Between 'De-Risking' and Drift Toward Beijing ↗
Europe entered 2026 in a 'do no harm' phase with China — cautious engagement replacing any real reset — after China's 2025 rare earth export restrictions left European factories cutting hours and delaying orders. Yet individual European leaders (UK PM Starmer, German Chancellor Merz, French President Macron) have all made high-level visits to Beijing seeking commercial deals, with Macron explicitly calling for more Chinese FDI in Europe. At the same time, the EU's €360B trade deficit with China hit a new record in 2025, the European Commission is debating deploying its Anti-Coercion 'trade bazooka,' and Brussels is proposing to phase out Huawei and ZTE from critical infrastructure. In May 2026, the EU and Japan also jointly met to reduce reliance on Chinese minerals, batteries, and clean technology.
↳ Europe is not converging on China — it is fracturing: Brussels hardening on trade instruments while individual capitals drift closer to Beijing for economic lifelines, a divide-and-rule dynamic that Beijing is actively exploiting and that directly affects Japan's own alliance calculus.
